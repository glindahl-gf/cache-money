Built For All Four · 04

What kind of property are you running?

Networks, studios, streamers, and live productions all run on talent. They don't run the same way. Pick yours — see the operation Greenfly runs underneath.

For TV networks

Every show. Every cast. Every drop. One stack.

Twelve scripted shows in primetime. Three reality franchises. A late-night. A morning show. Each with its own talent cohort, each with its own release rhythm. Greenfly is the layer that holds them all on the same operating standard — show-specific permissions, network-wide visibility, talent-level personalization.

  • Show-by-show roster — every cast, every host, every contributor, scoped per program.
  • Network-wide library — search across every show, every season, every promo.
  • Cross-promotion automation — late-night cuts feed primetime promo without a manual hand-off.
  • Affiliate-ready packages — 215 markets, localized variants, one push.
For studios

Premiere week to home video. One operation.

The world tour, the global rollout, the talent media training, the home-video drop, the streaming launch — all on a single operation, in 32 languages. The publicity team doesn't switch tools when the campaign moves from theatrical to streaming. The talent's reps don't relearn an approval flow.

  • 32-language packaging — global tentpoles, local-language cuts, per-territory rights.
  • Tour-mode — moving talent across markets, capturing every stop, syndicated to every outlet.
  • Theatrical-to-streaming continuity — same campaign, same stack, second window.
  • Awards-season operations — FYC pushes, talent-driven, nominee-aware, per-org compliant.
For streaming platforms

The talent feed and the platform feed, in sync.

Streaming runs on bingeable seasons and weekly drop episodes — and the platform's social calendar has to match the show's release calendar, every Thursday, all year. Greenfly is the engine that keeps talent-driven content moving in lockstep with platform-driven content. No manual sync. No gaps. No "the trailer dropped without the cast post."

  • Drop-day automation — talent fires the moment the episode goes live, not 40 minutes after.
  • Per-show cohort management — every show its own roster, every roster its own approval rules.
  • Window-aware rights — every clip carries the territory and window it's licensed in.
  • Platform + talent unification — the platform handle, the talent handle, the showrunner — all on one push.
For live productions

Awards. Halftime. Late-night. The same rig.

The night-of operation for a Grammys, a Super Bowl halftime, a late-night taping — multi-camera capture, per-talent rights, real-time approval, immediate syndication. Run-of-show cues fire content automatically. The star walks off stage with their post already queued and approved.

  • Multi-cam intake — every camera on the floor, into one bucket, tagged on landing.
  • Run-of-show triggers — content fires off the rundown, not the production assistant's stopwatch.
  • Per-talent permission — ribbon-cut talent gets ribbon-cut rights, performer gets performer rights.
  • Real-time syndication — outlets, talent, sponsors, all served the moment the moment happens.
