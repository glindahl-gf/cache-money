Built For All Four · 04

Whose press box are you running?

The night is different at a league office than it is at a team, a federation, or a collegiate program. The pipeline underneath isn't. Pick your floor — see the day Greenfly runs for it.

For league offices

Every team. Every game. One rights layer.

The league sees what every team produced, the second it lands. Sponsor delivery is centralized — one receipt, one report, one rights matrix. Conferences and broadcast partners pull from the same library the comms team posts from. The phrase "we'll get back to you on the sponsor frame" disappears.

  • League-wide library — every team's output, sortable by player, sponsor, partner, situation.
  • Centralized rights — one matrix. Every clip carries its rights with it.
  • Sponsor receipts — quarterly, monthly, or live. Whatever the deal said.
  • Broadcast partner ingest — they pull from your library, not your inbox.
For team comms & social

Your day fits inside the platform.

One window. One queue. Every sideline frame, every shoulder-cam clip, every player phone — landing in the same intake. Social fires from there. Sponsor fires from there. Player handles fire from there. The 14-row spreadsheet retires. The road trip stops eating Sundays.

  • Single intake — sideline, shoulder-cam, player phones, stadium feeds, all in one queue.
  • One-click cross-handle — team, league, sponsor, player, all from one approval.
  • Auto sponsor routing — frame recognized, frame delivered, brand team CC'd.
  • Player kits — every player's personal feed, branded, queued, scheduled.
For governing bodies

Protect the rights. Distribute the moment.

A federation's job is to protect the brand and the rights, in 200 markets, simultaneously. Greenfly is the rights layer that scales. One library, every confederation, every sanctioning body, every sponsor — under one rights regime. Tournaments push to confederations push to clubs push to players, with provenance preserved every step.

  • Multi-tier rights — federation → confederation → national → club → athlete.
  • Tournament-mode — single capture pool, multi-property distribution.
  • Provenance preserved — every share traced back to its origin license.
  • 32-language packaging — global tentpole, local-language drop, same pipeline.
For collegiate programs

  • Athlete-branded kits — weekly, post-game, season-finale, recruit-targeted.
  • Conference compliance — tag, route, log, every asset, every time.
