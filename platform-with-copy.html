01

Collect.

Every camera, every channel, every shooter — one inbox.

The shift begins where the content does: the moment a shutter clicks on a sideline, a backstage handler grabs a still off a wire, a brand partner ships a deliverable. Greenfly pulls all of it into a single, time-stamped pool — sideline, studio, press, talent, fan, wire — without anyone forwarding an email.

Sources are connected once, then ingest forever: agency drops, FTP feeds, mobile uploaders, signed-in shooters, partner APIs, photo wires, vendor delivery folders.

02

Identify. Tag. Organize.

Every frame, named. Every name, searchable.

Computer vision recognizes the faces, jerseys, logos, scenes, and moments inside every frame the second it lands. A taxonomy of athlete, partner, sponsor, scene, and event metadata gets attached automatically — and the asset slots itself into the right collection.

What used to be a librarian's job is now a continuous service. The pool is no longer raw — it's indexed.

04

Activate.

When the network lights up.

The moment an asset clears the routing engine, the network lights up: athletes post, partners deploy, sponsors trigger, retail studios update — within minutes of the moment itself. The shift's first big play is on the feed before the second is in the can.

Activation isn't a separate workflow. It's what happens when the rest of the arc arrives upstream of the people who'd otherwise be waiting.

Automate.

Humans create. Robots automate.

The arc closes. Identify feeds Tag. Tag feeds Organize. Organize feeds Distribute. Distribute drives Activate. Activate writes back into Measure. Measure tunes the next Identify pass — and the loop runs itself, under policy, without operators in the middle.

The shift you started by clocking in continues without you. You step in to create; everything between create and the audience is the platform.
